    The scourge of drugs against young people

    The situation of drug abuse among young people is now more and more common. From 2004 to now, the number is increasing. The number of people under the age of 20 is the most serious, which is of great concern to the community.
    Adolescents are in a period of physiological and psychological development, with weak psychological defense and strong curiosity; lack of ability to judge right and wrong, poor self-control; and vulnerable to peers. Therefore, many drug dealers will use lure, swaying and other means, and teenagers are the most vulnerable to being poisoned.
    Drug abuse can cause great harm to the health of adolescents, causing various diseases such as acute and chronic hepatitis, pneumonia, heart and kidney failure, etc., which can also cause brain damage, hallucinations, auditory hallucinations, schizophrenia, and even death. Secondly, the mental health of adolescents also has a great impact. Long-term drug use makes drug addicts addicted to the illusory feelings of floating, but they are unable to extricate themselves. They lose interest in interpersonal communication, love of life, can't concentrate on learning, and their performances plummet, affecting their studies. Even the future is completely destroyed. Moreover, the only thing that supports their bodies is drugs. When there is no money to buy, they will use any means to steal, rob, and even go to drug trafficking and embark on the road of breaking the law. Finally, drug addicts not only endanger themselves, but also worry family members, even destroy their relationship with their loved ones, and cause tragedies. It also means that individuals are on the path to destruction.
    For each person, life is only one time. Drug abuse is not only a harm but also a benefit. Young friends should deeply understand the scourge of drugs, build a strong psychological defense line, and establish correct values. In the face of temptation, we must resolutely reject the "first port", and we must be cautious in making friends, knowing how to say "no" and dispel the curiosity about drugs. We must cherish life and stay away from drugs.

     

    Ways to Refrain From Drug Abuse
    • Cultivate a happy family
    • Develop good hobbies e.g. drawing, singing
    • Participate in recreational activities
    • Express and ventilate your feelings
    • Share your problems with your family members, teachers or social workers
    • Keep away from all abused drugs 
    * Remember to say "NO" to these drugs when being offered

    Ten Ways to Say "NO"

    • Just simply say "NO"
    • Refuse strongly. "Don't push me...... I said No!"
    • Talk about the consequences of drug abuse. "This thing will make me sick"
    • Find an excuse to leave. "Sorry, I have to go for my tennis lesson now."
    • Talk about something else."Oh! I am going on a trip next week."
    • Suggest other options. "Here. Have some chocolates."
    • Refuse subtly."I am allergic to many drugs, so I can't take any without doctor's prescription."
    • Make friends carefully. Stay away from bad people. "You don't need to tempt me. None of my friends take them. It's more fun to play basketball."
    • Mention your parents."My dad won't let me take them."
    Mention professional advice."Drugs? Let me ask my doctor first."

     

    Harmful Effects of Drug Abuse

    • Contract diseases e.g. AIDS, hepatitis B and tetanus by sharing infected needles
    • Distorts perception of and response to the environment causing harm to oneself and to others
    • Alters behaviour
    • Adversely affects health and body functions e.g. brain, liver, lungs, kidneys, heart
    • Acute intoxication and death
    • Leads to poor appetite, malnutrition and weight loss
    • Become addicted
    • School and work performance may deteriorate
    • Since a lot of money is needed to buy drugs, one may commit crimes, become imprisoned and destroy one's future. Problems appear in interpersonal relationships and one can lose friends and even family
